Megaflo Unvented Boiler Systems: Everything You Need to Know

Understanding Megaflo: An Introduction

The Megaflo system, often mistakenly referred to as “Megaflow,” is a remarkable hot water cylinder made of stainless steel. As an unvented system, it stores and distributes hot water as part of your central heating system. It’s important to note that the Megaflo system is not a boiler replacement but rather a companion to a system boiler. By combining the power of a system boiler with a Megaflo cylinder, you can experience unparalleled hot water performance.

Is a Megaflo System Right for You?

To determine if a Megaflo system is suitable for your home, consider the following factors:

  1. High Hot Water Demand: If you frequently use multiple baths and showers simultaneously or require a high flow rate of over 16 liters per minute, a Megaflo system is ideal for meeting your hot water needs.
  2. Water Pressure: Megaflo systems thrive in households with high water pressure requirements. If you have high-pressure demands and ample storage space for the cylinder, a Megaflo system is an excellent choice.
  3. Hydrogen Ready Boiler: If you’re considering a new system boiler, ensure it is hydrogen-ready to comply with the gas boiler ban in 2025. Pairing a hydrogen-ready system boiler with a Megaflo cylinder ensures a future-proof and environmentally friendly heating solution.

Exploring Megaflo System Prices

When investing in a Megaflo system, it’s crucial to understand the associated costs. Here’s a breakdown of the expenses you can expect:

  1. Megaflo Cylinder: The cost of a Megaflo cylinder, also known as a Megaflo tank, typically ranges from £500 to £1500. Prices may vary depending on the capacity and specific model you choose.
  2. Megaflo System Boiler: A Megaflo system boiler paired with an unvented cylinder can cost between £1,000 and £3,500. Be sure to inquire whether the price includes VAT and installation fees.
  3. Installation Costs: Unvented hot water cylinder installation can range from £500 to £2,000, depending on the complexity of the work required for the boiler installation. It’s advisable to obtain a detailed quote from a qualified installer.

Differentiating Between Indirect and Direct Unvented Megaflo Systems

Megaflo systems come in two variations: indirect and direct. Let’s explore their differences:

  1. Indirect Unvented Megaflo: In this configuration, the hot water supplied by an external source, such as a regular or system boiler, heats the water stored in the cylinder. The cylinder’s coil ensures the water temperature remains consistent based on the thermostat settings. When hot water is used, the boiler refills the cylinder to maintain an ample supply.
  2. Direct Unvented Megaflo: This type of Megaflo system utilizes the mains cold water supply as its primary source. Inside the cylinder, a Megaflo immersion heater switch, powered by electricity, heats the water to the desired temperature defined by the thermostat. When hot water is drawn, the system replenishes the supply with cold water from the main feed.that both indirect and direct unvented Megaflo systems offer reliable and efficient hot water solutions for your home. The choice between the two depends on your specific requirements and preferences.

Advantages of a Megaflo Boiler System

Investing in a Megaflo system comes with several benefits, including:

  1. Space-saving Design: Megaflo systems eliminate the need for a large loft-installed tank, saving valuable space in your home. With no requirement for a cold water tank, you have more flexibility in choosing the installation location for your Megaflo cylinder.
  2. Easy Installation and Maintenance: Megaflo systems are designed for easy installation, and their components are readily available for replacement and maintenance. Regularly inspecting and maintaining the system ensures optimal performance and longevity.
  3. Compatibility with Renewable Energy: In the event of a boiler breakdown, the Megaflo system is compatible with solar PV, providing a renewable energy source and ensuring a backup solution for hot water needs.
  4. Enhanced Water Pressure: Megaflo systems utilize mains pressure, resulting in improved water pressure for your showers and baths. Even in busy households with multiple outlets in use simultaneously, the Megaflo system delivers a satisfying high-pressure shower experience.
  5. Safety Features: Megaflo systems incorporate various safety features, such as a cylinder thermostat that shuts down at the correct temperature and a Temperature/Pressure Relief Valve that regulates pressure by releasing water if necessary. These safety measures provide peace of mind for homeowners.

Considerations and Limitations

While Megaflo systems offer numerous advantages, it’s important to be aware of some considerations:

  1. Mains Water Pressure: If your mains water pressure is low, it may impact the water pressure delivered by the Megaflo system. To assess suitability, consider having a flow rate test conducted by engineers to determine if the system can meet your requirements.
  2. Potential Noise: The high velocity of water flowing through the pipes in a Megaflo system can generate noise. Proper inspection of the existing pipework’s capability to withstand the pressure is essential. Replacing pipes to accommodate a Megaflo system can incur additional costs.
  3. Continuous Water Supply: As Megaflo systems rely on the mains water supply, a consistent water pressure is necessary to ensure an uninterrupted hot water supply. Any interruptions in the mains water flow may result in a temporary shortage of hot water.
